Who are we?

The Night Owls are a Robotics Club focusing on bringing new people into the world of robotics. Our team competes in the FTC (First Tech Challenge) annually helping our team to learn skills in competition and team work.

contact us

First Competition

The First Tech Challenge is a worldwide robotics competition that tests problem solving and quick thinking.

Engineering and Robot Design

Students learn how to design 3D models, build robots, and brainstorm ideas with the team. 

Programming

When joining the team, members will be able to learn the basics of coding and slowly build up knowledge even if they have no prior experience.

Our Club

What makes us special?

Free to Join

Our team is ASB-funded and has no cost to enlist in the team. This makes it easier for people to join and get the experience. Anyone from our school, Pacific Crest Innovation Academy is invited to join.

Our School

Flexible and Diverse

Whatever skill level you may be, you are welcome. Our club is designed to accommodate the beginners and the veterans. We do this by using a two-team format, allowing for both to get the most out of their time.

Designing Better Futures

Our group leaves members with good skillsets that can be implemented in later life needs. This includes teamwork, rational thinking, and competition.

What is our School?

PCIA or Pacific Crest Innovation Academy is a small free public high school program and an amazing place to learn and grow while preparing for college, career, and beyond. Go to pcia.org to learn more.

How do we run?

Our club is a ASB (Associated Student Body) run club and is separate from the school as a team as we previously were. We are commonly compiled of two teams of different levels. Being team #15324 and #24211 in FTC (First Tech Challenge). 

Where will your Donations go?

Your donations will go towards benefiting our team in many ways. This includes getting our team new tech. In the world of growth, tech changes and improvements are always happening. Another location funding will be distributed is helping our team compete in FTC. The course and registration alone is costly and yearly.
